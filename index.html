Biography

When I was eight years old, my mom decided that I should pick up either the violin or the piano. I didn’t know what either of those words meant, so I went with the one I heard first. Thanks, mom!

After years of classical training, I grew fond of the violin, but along the way I fell in love with a dream much greater: learning every instrument I could. Since then I’ve picked up the violin, viola, cello, double bass, trombone, and piano, and it’s been the greatest gift of my musical career—especially taking the plunge of switching over to viola.

Somehow one thing led to another, and I started traveling the world performing in places like Costa Rica’s National Theater and Taiwan’s Presidential Office Building. Back at home in Los Angeles, I’ve played in everything from short films and Broadway musicals to string quartets and symphony orchestras.

But at the end of the day, I still feel like that same eight-year-old kid at heart, noodling around on random instruments for hours on end and trying to use music to navigate this crazy ride we call life. Thanks for joining me!

The Jeff Guarantee

A Unique Sound

Compared to our violinist counterparts, viola players are few and far between. As a result, booking a violist is a surefire way to make your wedding or event even more special. Our signature sound carries both the elegance of a violin and the warmth of a cello, all in one package.

A Well-Rounded Background

Learning multiple instruments in a variety of settings has put me in a unique position to quickly perform a wide range of music, from big band hits to musical numbers. That's why I'm happy to work with you to create a fully customizable setlist for your wedding or event.

Thanks to my experience event planning for the LA creative community and my day job as a graphic designer, you can rest easy knowing I have the communication and coordination skills that go into pulling off a vision as singular as yours. Plus, it means I'll show up in a great outfit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Logistics

How much do you charge?

I don't list any prices here because your quote will depend on a few factors, primarily performance length, travel time, and amount of custom song requests.

That being said, I want to work within your budget as much as possible, which is why I'm proud to offer price matching with proof of another musician's quote for comparable services!

Once we've agreed on a quote, a 25% deposit is required to confirm of your booking. I typically handle payments through Venmo, Zelle, or PayPal.

What's your cancellation/reschedule policy?

Your 25% deposit will be forfeited in the event of cancellation, unless proof is provided of an emergency which prevents the event from happening altogether.

I am happy to reschedule bookings at no extra cost.

Are you willing to travel?

Yes! I'm based in Los Angeles, but I'm happy to go wherever on the planet your event takes us. An additional fee may apply for anything past an hour of travel.

How long can you play for?

I don't have a strict minimum or maximum length!

Note that for bookings 2 hours or longer, I reserve the right to take a short 5–10 minute break after each hour of playing, during which I'll play canned music through my sound setup.

Do you have vendor insurance?

Yes! I have general liability insurance up to $1M, and am happy to provide a copy if your venue requires it.

Do you bring your own sound equipment?

Yes! I have my own sound setup, which includes the option to amplify both my instrument and my backing tracks.

What will you wear?

I dress cocktail attire for most performances, but if your dress code calls for something else, I'm happy to oblige!

Music

Can you play a different instrument?

I can play violin, electric 5-string violin, or cello by request. Please be advised that some pieces in my repertoire may not be available on these instruments.

Can you play with a group?

I can play in a duo, trio, or quartet with other strings and/or a piano by request. Please be advised that some pieces in my repertoire may not be available with groups.

Do you use backing tracks?

Yes! I use backing tracks to add more dimension to the music. Depending on the song and on your event needs, this will either be the full instrumental track from the original song, or a stripped piano/guitar/strings track.

Can you learn a song for my event?

Yes! If you don't see what you're looking for in my repertoire, I would be happy to learn any and all songs you'd like. A small additional fee may apply.

Can you help me choose songs?

Yes! If you'd like some help topping off your setlist, or if you just have a general vibe in mind, I'd be happy to work with you to fill in the gaps based on my recommendations.
